# Contributing to AI UX Playbook

This playbook is built from direct production experience. Contributions grounded in real AI product deployments are welcome.

## What kinds of contributions are useful

**New patterns** — AI-native UX patterns not covered in the current library, with a real product example and design mandates. The bar: must come from production experience or validated research, not theoretical reasoning.

**Pattern extensions** — additional examples, edge cases, or domain-specific adaptations for existing patterns.

**Anti-pattern additions** — documented anti-patterns from real products, with evidence of the failure mode and its cost. The anti-patterns section is the most valuable part of this playbook for practitioners — additions here are especially welcome.

**Real product examples** — analysis of how specific AI products implement (or fail to implement) the patterns documented here. Must be based on direct product use, not secondary reporting.

**Translations** — translations of the README into other languages go in `/translations/README.[lang].md`.

## What to avoid

- Patterns based on theoretical scenarios without production or research grounding
- Anti-patterns without evidence of real-world failure
- Content that recommends hiding AI limitations from users

## How to contribute

1. Fork the repo
2. Create a branch: `git checkout -b your-contribution-name`
3. Make your changes
4. Open a pull request with context on where the pattern or example comes from
